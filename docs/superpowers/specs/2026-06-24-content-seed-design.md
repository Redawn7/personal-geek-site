# Personal Site Content Seed Design

## Goal

Make the site feel lived-in by adding believable, editable first-person drafts based on five user-approved themes: Melbourne study life, Tokyo travel, skiing, Shanghai food, and recent reading.

The writing uses simple English as the main language with short Chinese passages for warmth and clarity. It should sound personal without inventing sensitive or precise facts.

## Editorial Rules

- Write in first person as an editable draft.
- Keep school names, exact addresses, companions, prices, and exact travel dates unspecified.
- Do not invent awards, achievements, dramatic events, or private relationships.
- Do not use stock photos as if they were the author's own photographs.
- Prefer concrete observations, small emotions, and useful reflections over travel-guide language.
- Keep English understandable for a middle-school reader; use Chinese where nuance would otherwise be lost.

## Content Set

### Blog Posts

1. `Learning to Live in Melbourne`
   A reflective study-abroad story about weather, trams, cooking, independence, and slowly feeling at home.
2. `Tokyo in Small Details`
   A travel story built around trains, convenience stores, quiet streets, signs, and the rhythm of walking.
3. `My First Honest Day on Snow`
   A skiing story about falling, balance, fear, repetition, and the joy of one clean turn.
4. `Eating My Way Through Shanghai`
   A food story about noodles, breakfast, late-night meals, and how food becomes a map of the city.

### Reading Note

5. `Reading Slowly Again`
   A note about reading with fewer highlights, keeping one useful idea, and letting books change daily choices rather than only producing quotes.

### Life Postcards

Create seven short entries:

- Two Melbourne fragments: a tram-window morning and cooking after class.
- Two Tokyo fragments: an early station and a quiet side street at night.
- One skiing fragment: the moment before starting down the slope.
- Two Shanghai food fragments: breakfast and a late-night bowl of noodles.

Each postcard includes a broad city or activity location, weather, mood, tags, and a short bilingual body. No coordinates are stored.

### Now Update

Update the Now page to show:

- Making: filling the Personal Signal Atlas with real stories.
- Reading: reading slowly and keeping fewer, better notes.
- Thinking: how places become memories through small repeated details.

## Homepage Result

The homepage should naturally show three Life Postcards, a fuller latest-updates list, and richer content counters without changing the existing layout. The archive, blog, life, and notes routes gain enough material to feel intentional rather than empty.

## Images

This phase uses the existing image fallback treatment. Real personal photos can be added later by placing files in the site and adding their paths to each Life entry. This avoids presenting generated or stock images as real memories.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Four new published posts, one published reading note, and seven new published Life entries exist.
- Now content reflects the new editorial direction.
- All frontmatter validates against the existing Astro content schemas.
- Writing is primarily simple English with meaningful Chinese support.
- No exact private location, coordinates, fabricated institution, or fabricated restaurant appears.
- Tests and production build pass.
