I used to think a good reading session should produce many highlights. The more lines I saved, the more useful the book seemed.

But a long list of highlights often became another thing I never opened again. I am trying a slower method now.

## Highlight less

I wait before marking a sentence. If the idea still feels important after the next page, I keep it. This small pause helps me separate surprise from value.

## Keep one idea

After reading, I write down one idea in my own words. One clear thought is easier to remember than ten copied sentences. It also shows whether I understood the idea or only liked how it sounded.

## Test it in ordinary life

The best notes should return to the real world. I ask whether an idea can change how I plan tomorrow, speak to someone, or spend my attention. If nothing changes, the note may still be interesting, but it is not yet useful.
